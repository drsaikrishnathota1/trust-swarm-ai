# RA-MARS Reference List

## 
## Working Title
RA-MARS: A Resilient AI-Driven Mission Assurance Framework for Secure Multi-UAV Defence Surveillance Under Jamming, GPS Spoofing, and Data-Tampering Attacks

## Reference Collection Target
Total target references: 45–70

Minimum recent references:
- 25 or more from 2021–2026
- Prefer 2023–2026 papers where possible
- Include high-quality journal and conference papers
- Avoid relying too heavily on low-quality or non-indexed sources

## Reference Categories

### Category 1: UAV Defence Surveillance and Reconnaissance
Target: 8–10 papers

Search keywords:
- multi-UAV defence surveillance
- UAV reconnaissance defence applications
- unmanned aerial vehicle battlefield surveillance
- drone swarm surveillance military
- UAV critical infrastructure protection
- autonomous UAV defence operations

Notes:
Focus on papers that explain why UAVs are useful in defence, surveillance, reconnaissance, and battlefield awareness.

### Category 2: Multi-UAV Mission Planning and Mission Assurance
Target: 6–8 papers

Search keywords:
- multi-UAV mission planning
- UAV mission assurance
- resilient autonomous systems
- UAV swarm mission coordination
- autonomous mission continuity UAV
- multi-agent UAV task allocation

Notes:
These papers will support the mission-continuation and mission-risk part of RA-MARS.

### Category 3: RF Jamming Detection and Anti-Jamming in UAV Networks
Target: 8–10 papers

Search keywords:
- UAV jamming detection
- anti-jamming UAV communication
- RF jamming drone networks
- UAV communication under jamming
- cyber electromagnetic attacks UAV
- resilient UAV communication jamming

Notes:
Focus on papers with measurable metrics such as packet delivery ratio, latency, communication reliability, and attack detection.

## Literature Review Matrix Fields
For each paper, record:

- Full citation
- Year
- Venue/journal
- Problem addressed
- Method used
- Dataset/simulation used
- Metrics used
- Strengths
- Limitations
- Relevance to RA-MARS
- Which gap it supports

## Priority Source Types
Prefer:
- Defence Technology
- IEEE journals and conferences
- Elsevier journals
- Springer journals
- ACM papers
- MDPI Sensors / Drones only if technically strong
- arXiv only when supported by peer-reviewed sources

## What to Avoid
- Do not collect only review papers
- Do not rely on weak or unrelated blockchain papers
- Do not include papers only because they mention UAV
- Do not include outdated references unless foundational
- Do not use fake or unverifiable references
- Do not overuse quantum references for this article

## Minimum Requirement Before Manuscript Submission
Before submission, the paper should include:
- At least 45 references
- At least 25 recent references from 2021–2026
- At least 10 references directly related to jamming and spoofing
- At least 8 references related to AI-based anomaly or attack detection
- At least 5 references related to mission assurance or resilient autonomy
- At least 5 references related to tamper-resistant logging or data integrity
