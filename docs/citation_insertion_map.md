# TRUST-Swarm Citation Insertion Map

## Purpose

This file maps candidate references to the correct manuscript sections. The goal is to avoid random citation placement and make the final manuscript look professional, organized, and journal-ready.

Before final submission, each citation must be verified for exact title, venue, year, DOI, and publisher status.

## Citation Placement Rule

Avoid placing too many citations after every sentence.

Better style:

* 2–4 citations after a major claim paragraph
* 1–2 citations after a method description
* 3–5 citations in dense related-work comparison paragraphs

Avoid:

* citation dumping
* citing arXiv-only references too heavily
* citing Wikipedia or blogs
* citing unverified future-dated references in final submission

---

## Final Citation Audit Rule

Before final paper submission, each candidate citation must be checked for:

1. exact author order
2. exact paper title
3. venue or journal
4. year
5. DOI or arXiv ID
6. peer-reviewed status
7. relevance to the cited sentence

Only audited references should remain in the final manuscript.
